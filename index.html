Madeira Island

Discover about Pearl of the Atlantic

Fun Facts

Porto Moniz Natural Pools

Madeira has volcanic lava pools on the north coast that are filled by the Atlantic sea.

Laurissilva Forest

This UNESCO World Heritage laurel forest is over 10 million years old and covers nearly 20% of the island.

Gastronomy

Madeira Wine

The island is famous for its fortified wine, which can age for decades and is still enjoyed worldwide.

Bolo do Caco

A traditional Madeiran flatbread usually served warm with garlic butter.

Espetada

Skewered beef rubbed with bay leaves and grilled over wood, a local BBQ favorite.

Curiosities

Levadas

Madeira has over 2,000 km of levadas (irrigation canals) that double as scenic hiking trails.

Miniature Golf Capital

Funchal is home to one of the world’s largest miniature golf courses, spanning 8,000m².

Windmills and Whales

The island’s east coast is famous for windmills, and the surrounding waters are great for spotting whales and dolphins.
